Vigenère Cipher

What it is: A polyalphabetic cipher that uses a keyword to encrypt the text, where each letter in the plaintext is shifted by different amounts based on the keyword.
Key usage: The key is a word or phrase, and each letter of the key determines how much to shift the corresponding letter in the plaintext.
Why it’s stronger than Caesar: The varying shifts based on the keyword make it harder to detect patterns, but it's still breakable with frequency analysis and modern cryptanalysis methods.
