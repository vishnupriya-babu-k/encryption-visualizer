Caesar Cipher

What it is: A simple, classical encryption technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet.
Key usage: The key is the shift value (e.g., shifting by 3 moves 'A' to 'D').
Why it’s easy to break: It only has 25 possible keys, making it vulnerable to brute-force attacks and frequency analysis.
