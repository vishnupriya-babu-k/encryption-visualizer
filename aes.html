AES Cipher

What it is: A widely-used modern encryption standard that encrypts data in blocks using a symmetric key algorithm.
Key usage: AES uses 128, 192, or 256-bit keys to encrypt data, with multiple rounds of complex transformations to scramble the information.
Why it’s hard to break:Why it’s hard to break: AES is extremely secure, and brute-force attacks are computationally infeasible. It is resistant to known cryptanalysis and theoretical attacks, making it the industry standard for data encryption.
